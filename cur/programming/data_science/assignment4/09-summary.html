This brings us to the end of our assignments. By now you have learned some very important ideas in data science and you have also learned some very valuable programming skills that help reinforce those ideas.

Always feel free to (safely) download datasets from trusted websites and do some data science of your own using the skills you have learned. You probably know more than you think you do, and you have the power to do some very cool things with the knowledge you have!

It is also important to remember that these skills can be used to convince people of certain patterns or trends in data, so always make sure you are using your skillset wisely and responsibly in a way that does not intend to mislead or deceive people with data (if you have ever seen an article along the lines of "Here's how eating chocolate every day can extend your life by 15 years!...", you are looking at an example of someone conducting unethical data science). Your conclusions about data always need to be reinforced by irrefutable facts, not gut feelings or desires to see the data reflect a certain pattern. Always Data Science responsibly!

We encourage you to continue your data science education at the university level if you have the opportunity. Classes like Data 8 at UC Berkeley, and many other classes like it at countless other universities are a great way to expand your data science knowledge and explore your interest!
