As Joey Gonzalez, creator of UC Berkeley's Data 100 course puts it: "Data Science is the application of data centric, computational, and inferential thinking to understand the world and solve problems". What that essentially means is that data science involves learning about the world by asking and answering questions using data.

In order to best use data science principles, we want to be able to think in a computational way. Computational thinking is about formulating things with enough clarity, and in a systematic enough way, that one can tell a computer how to do them. This does not necessarily mean thinking exactly like a computer; in fact, computational thinking is often a skill heavily aided by creativity and outside-the-box ideas.

To engage with data science principles, we will be using a Data Science library designed specifically for Snap! known as DASIS (DAta Science In Snap!). Using DASIS, we will progressively add more and more tools to our data science "toolkit" until you can use all your tools to work on datasets and ask data-driven questions of your own!
