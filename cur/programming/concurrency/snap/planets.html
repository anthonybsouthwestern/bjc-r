While you have already used multiple sprites, such as when making a Kaleidoscope, now you will have multiple sprites with customized costumes that move independently of each other.

  • You will need to add sprites to your project. You can use between the stage and the sprite corral, at the center right of the window. Alternatively, you can right-click on a sprite to duplicate it, and then make changes to the one you created.
  • Each sprite will have separate costumes and scripting areas: click on the image in the sprite corral to focus on it. To add costumes, you just need to drag an image from a page in your browser over to the scripting area of your snap window. Here are costumes you should use, with labels to their right and the x and y coordinates where they should be placed originally. You can right-click on an image to download it to your machine:
    Sun (1, 1) Mercury (30, 3) Venus (46, 0) Earth (60, 2) Mars (75, 1) Jupiter (100, 7) Saturn (132, -1) Uranus (160, -2) Neptune (197, -1)
  • Place the sun and planets at the following locations. One way to do so is by using the block. It may also be helpful to use the sensor blocks seen in a previous lab to determine if they are placed appropriately. You may use any reasonable approach that has the sun and all planets placed appropriately when the lab is opened.
  • Each sprite will have different scripts. To switch between the scripts and costumes for a sprite, use the tabs at the top of the scripting area.
  • Go to the Backgrounds tab when you have the Stage selected (located similarly to where you find Costumes when you have a sprite selected) and create an all-black background. If you later wish to add stars, you may.
