You will now have each planet move to orbit the sun, drawing a line showing its path. The procedure will be the same for each planet, but the amount and direction that each move will vary.

Recall the Snap! program you saw in Welcome to Snap.:



You will be placing a block with similar components for each planet.

  • Each planet will need the hat block for when the green flag is clicked, which will then control the planet's movement and the drawing of its orbit.
  • Before any drawing or movement happens, clear any existing path that is drawn.
  • Set the pen color for the orbit for each planet to be a color that is as similar as possible to the most predominant color on the planet image. You need not be overly precise, but the path for Earth shouldn't be red!
  • Put the pen down.
  • Use the forever block which you saw in Randomly moving a character to continuously move the sprite and draw its orbit. See below for the number of steps to move and the degrees to turn (clockwise) for each planet. These are not guaranteed to accurately represent planetary movement, but should have the planets orbiting the sun.
    Mercury: move 5 steps, turn 10 degrees
    Venus: move 7 steps, turn 9 degrees
    Earth: move 10 steps, turn 10 degrees
    Mars: move 10 steps, turn 8 degrees
    Jupiter: move 10 steps, turn 6 degrees
    Saturn: move 10 steps, turn 4.5 degrees
    Uranus: move 10 steps, turn 3.7 degrees
    Neptune: move 10 steps, turn 3 degrees
  • Optional challenge: Without changing the components above, add in additional space features, such as moons of planets or other celestial bodies.
